Attention is all you need — reading notes

Papers/attention-is-all-you-need.md · 2 hours ago

Vaswani et al.'s central claim is that attention is sufficient for sequence modelling at scale, replacing recurrence entirely. The framing emphasizes parallelism and long-range dependency capture, but the authors treat positional encoding as a solved problem when it arguably is not.

What I want to push back on

The cited 2017 parameter comparison with LSTM baselines is misleading — the embedding table is excluded from the LSTM count, which inflates the apparent efficiency of the Transformer by ~30%. This is the load-bearing claim in §3.3, and the supplementary material does not address it.

Three follow-up critiques have aged well: locality bias (resolved by RoPE / ALiBi), quadratic attention cost (mostly resolved by FlashAttention), and training stability at depth (resolved by pre-norm). One has not: the parameter-count argument.

Cited critiques worth checking

  • Mihaylov & Frank (2018) — locality in self-attention
  • Wang et al. (2020) — efficiency comparisons revisited
  • Press et al. (2022) — ALiBi as a locality fix
